Community Guidelines

CI Needs is built on trust, respect, and mutual support. These guidelines exist to keep our community safe and welcoming for every Dolphin.

Our Values

CI Needs exists because we believe every student deserves the support they need to stay in school and thrive. This platform is built on three core values:

  • Dignity — Every person using this platform deserves to be treated with respect, regardless of what they need or what they have to offer.
  • Safety — We protect our community from scams, harassment, and harmful content. If something feels wrong, flag it.
  • Community — We are all Dolphins. The strength of CI Needs comes from students looking out for each other.

Posting Rules

To keep the feed useful and trustworthy, all posts must follow these rules:

  • Be honest and accurate. Only post needs or offerings that are real. Do not exaggerate, misrepresent, or post on behalf of someone else without their knowledge.
  • Use the correct category. Categorize your post accurately so students searching for specific help can find it easily.
  • Be specific. Include enough detail for someone to actually help — item condition, size, quantity, pickup location preferences, etc.
  • Keep it relevant. Posts must be related to genuine basic needs. This is not a general buy/sell/trade marketplace.
  • One post per need. Do not post the same need multiple times. Edit your existing post if your situation changes.
  • Mark fulfilled posts. When your need has been met or your offering has been claimed, please mark the post as fulfilled so other students know it is no longer available.
  • Posts expire after 4 weeks. You will receive a reminder email before your post is removed. Respond to keep it active.

Good example: "Offering: lightly used Dell laptop charger (65W USB-C), works with Dell Inspiron 2020–2023. Free to pick up near the Bell Tower, available weekday afternoons."

Not allowed: "Selling MacBook Pro $800" — CI Needs is not a for-profit marketplace. Posts must be free offerings or genuine needs.

Respectful Interactions

All communication on CI Needs — in posts, comments, and direct messages — must be respectful and constructive.

  • Be kind. Students posting needs may be in a difficult situation. Respond with empathy and without judgment.
  • Comments must be helpful. Only comment if you have something useful to contribute — a resource, an offer, relevant information, or a supportive message.
  • No pressure or guilt. Do not pressure posters to respond quickly, share personal information, or accept your offer.
  • Follow through. If you commit to helping someone, follow through. Leaving someone waiting for help they were counting on is harmful.
  • Respect privacy. Do not share or repost anyone's personal information, contact details, or post content outside of CI Needs without their explicit permission.

Prohibited Content

The following are not permitted on CI Needs and will result in immediate post removal and possible account action:

  • Scams or fraudulent offers. Any post designed to deceive, collect personal information dishonestly, or take advantage of students in need.
  • Harassment or hate speech. Content that targets, demeans, or discriminates against any person or group based on race, gender, religion, sexuality, disability, national origin, or any other characteristic.
  • Commercial solicitation. Advertising products or services for profit, recruiting for paid programs, or promoting third-party services.
  • Illegal items or activities. Posts involving controlled substances, weapons, stolen property, or any activity that violates local, state, or federal law.
  • Spam or duplicate posts. Posting the same content repeatedly or flooding the feed with irrelevant content.
  • False reports. Deliberately flagging posts that do not violate guidelines in order to suppress legitimate content or target another user.
  • Impersonation. Pretending to be another student, faculty member, or CSUCI staff in any post, comment, or message.

Posts containing prohibited content will be removed immediately. Serious or repeated violations may result in permanent account suspension and referral to CSUCI Student Conduct.

Flagging Posts

If you see a post that you believe violates these guidelines, please use the Flag Post button on the post card. Flagging is anonymous — the poster will not know who flagged their post.

When you flag a post, you will be asked to:

  • Select a reason from the provided list
  • Optionally leave a brief comment to help our team understand the issue

Flagged posts are reviewed by CI Needs administrators. Posts that are found to violate these guidelines will be removed. Posts that are found to be in compliance will remain active.

Please flag responsibly. Misusing the flag system to target other users is itself a violation of these guidelines and may result in account action.

Consequences for Violations

CI Needs uses a tiered response system. The severity of the action taken depends on the nature and frequency of the violation.

First Offense
Warning
Post removed, user notified with explanation and link to guidelines
Repeated Violations
Temporary Suspension
Account placed on hold for 7–30 days, all posts hidden during suspension
Severe / Persistent
Permanent Ban
Account permanently disabled, possible referral to CSUCI Student Conduct

Certain violations — such as scams targeting students in need, hate speech, or illegal content — may result in immediate permanent suspension without prior warning.

Appeals

If you believe your post was removed in error or your account was actioned unfairly, you have the right to appeal. To submit an appeal:

  • Contact the CI Needs team using the contact form below
  • Include your CI email address and a brief explanation of why you believe the action was made in error
  • Appeals are reviewed within 3–5 business days

All appeal decisions are final. We are committed to reviewing every appeal fairly and promptly.

Questions or Concerns?

If you have questions about these guidelines or want to report an issue that isn't covered by the flag system, please reach out to the CI Needs team directly at cineeds@csuci.edu or by phone at (805) 555-1212.

For urgent safety concerns involving a student in crisis, please contact CSUCI Campus Police at (805) 437-8444 or visit Emergency Info.
